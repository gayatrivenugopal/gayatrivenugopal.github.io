Research

My doctoral research focused on the application of natural language processing and supervised classification techniques to enhance text accessibility in Hindi. Key areas of my research include:

  • Natural Language Processing: Identifying complex words in Hindi text and providing simpler alternatives to enhance readability.
  • Data Science and Machine Learning: Using supervised classification techniques for word sense disambiguation.
  • Educational Technology: Published studies in international journals and conference proceedings.
I earned my Ph.D. from Symbiosis International University, India, with a thesis on Automated Lexical Simplification of Hindi Text. The objective of the research was to improve the accessibility of text in Hindi by automating the identification of complex, that is, difficult to understand words, and providing simpler alternatives to the target complex words. A significant of this research was sponsored by Symbiosis Centre for Research and Innovation, Symbiosis International University, and approved by the Independent Ethics Committee, Symbiosis International University.
The project involved creating language resources, developing models for identifying complex words, and devising and implementing word sense disambiguation techniques for selecting appropriate simpler synonyms.

Methodology

  1. Created a Hindi aesthetics corpus and stop word list
  2. Developed an annotated dataset (CWID-hi) for complex word identification
  3. Used machine learning models (Random Forest, Extra Trees, AdaBoost, XGBoost, Gradient Boosting) for complex word identification
  4. Implemented a knowledge-based word sense disambiguation method using BabelNet and contextualized embeddings

Key Contributions and Findings

  • Designed and executed a comprehensive annotation study, and engineered novel language resources for Hindi, including a stop lemma list, aesthetics corpus, and the CWID-hi dataset, demonstrating expertise in data collection, cleaning, engineering, and corpus linguistics.
  • Devised and tuned classification models for complex word identification and lexical simplification in Hindi.
  • Conducted in-depth data analysis to provide key insights into factors influencing word complexity in Hindi.
  • Used feature engineering methods to identify key predictors of word complexity, with frequency emerging as the most impactful.
  • Leveraged ensemble machine learning models to achieve promising results in Hindi complex word identification.
  • Experimented with and successfully implemented word sense disambiguation for Hindi using ARES sense embeddings.
  • Advanced the field of text simplification for Hindi, with potential applications in education, healthcare, and accessibility.
